About This Guide

The project

This guide was developed to help scholars in the humanities and social sciences engage critically and effectively with large language models (LLMs). It accompanies a hands-on workshop but is designed to be equally useful as a standalone resource.

The content reflects the state of these technologies as of early 2026. LLM capabilities change rapidly; specific model names and features may be outdated by the time you read this. The broader principles — about verification, ethical awareness, and informed decision-making — should remain relevant longer than any particular technical detail.

A note on how this was made: Using AI to produce a guide about AI is itself a demonstration of the technology in action — including its limitations. The human author is responsible for all editorial decisions, factual claims, and errors.

Workshops

An associated workshop, built around the material in this guide, can be arranged for departments, research groups, or other institutional contexts. Sessions can be tailored to specific disciplines and levels of prior experience. Please get in touch if you would like to discuss this.

Adapting the guide

The guide is built to be adaptable. Discipline-specific examples and institution-specific information can be configured via URL parameters, making it straightforward to offer a version tailored to a particular department or university. If you are interested in adapting this guide for your own discipline or institution, please contact Leif Isaksen for further information.
