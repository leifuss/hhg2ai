# Philosophy

If you work in Philosophy, the AI-assisted tasks most relevant to your work are close reading and argument analysis, engagement with secondary literature, and teaching preparation. Philosophy's emphasis on precision of argument, the structure of reasoning, and the careful handling of conceptual distinctions makes it both a natural testing ground for AI tools and a discipline where their limitations are quickly exposed.

## Suggested reading order

1. **Start here:** [Close Reading & Textual Analysis](../close-reading.md) — using AI for argument analysis, conceptual mapping, and engagement with philosophical texts.

2. [Teaching & Assessment](../teaching.md) — preparing seminars, tutorials, and assessments. Particularly relevant for designing tasks that require genuine philosophical reasoning.

See also: [Translation & Language](../translation.md) if you work with texts in ancient or modern languages, and [Archives & Manuscripts](../archives.md) if you work with historical philosophical manuscripts.
