# Classics & Ancient History

If you work in Classics or Ancient History, the AI-assisted tasks you are most likely to encounter are translation and language work, analysing images of inscriptions or objects, working with fragmentary or lacunose texts, and spatial analysis of the ancient world. Close reading and teaching preparation are also relevant across the discipline.

## Suggested reading order

1. **Start here:** [Translation & Language](../translation.md) — covers ancient language work, checking model output against grammars and lexica, and the specific pitfalls of working with Greek, Latin, and other classical languages.

2. [Images, Objects & Visual Evidence](../images-objects.md) — working with photographs of inscriptions, pottery, coins, and archaeological material.

3. [Close Reading & Textual Analysis](../close-reading.md) — using AI for commentary, argument analysis, and engagement with secondary literature.

4. [Spatial & Geographic Analysis](../spatial.md) — mapping, gazetteers, and geographic reasoning about the ancient world.

5. [Teaching & Assessment](../teaching.md) — preparing seminars, tutorials, and assessments.

See also: [Quantitative & Tabular Data](../data.md) if you work with catalogues, databases, or statistical evidence.
