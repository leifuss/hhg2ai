Claude AI Experiments

A collection of experiments and small projects using Claude AI

Some of these have been used for teaching or research, others are simply a proof of concept. The intention is to give colleagues an idea of some of the capabilities of working with Large Language Models and AI agents. Most were developed in under one day, though some build on more extensive prior work.

For an informed-choice guide to LLMs aimed at humanities scholars, see the LLM Guide for Humanities Scholars.

Non-coding

CLA3131 QGIS Tutorials for Ancient History

Standard QGIS tutorials were redeveloped and adapted automatically to work with case studies relevant to ancient historians. These were written to RST format so that professional quality tutorials could be seamlessly embedded within the QGIS documentation interface.

The Lares

A comic short story that imagines the societal consequences of a Classicist and a Theologian discovering an unexpected form of intelligence. Collaboratively authored with an LLM (January 2025).

Coding

Gnomon Shadow Explorer

An interactive demo to show how latitude can be calculated using a gnomon. Developed in 1–2 hours in January 2026.

Online RTI Viewer

A usable online viewer for Reflectance Transformation Image files.

Grammar Crammer

An experimental platform for targeted practice of Latin grammar. Learners take a rapid diagnostic test of key grammatical concepts, which identifies areas most requiring practice. They are then presented with relevant exercises for practice and production, followed by an exit quiz to test improvement, and final report. Cookies allow for improvement to be tracked over multiple iterations.

RavenC:LAW

An interactive map and directory, comparing place references across the Ravenna Cosmography and Peutinger Map. The underlying data and principal coding were done manually over the course of 3 months in 2025. The codebase was subsequently improved in September 2025, with AI-assisted bugfixes, performance optimisations and interface improvements.

Interactive Ancient Globes

An interactive set of ancient Greek and Islamic globes demonstrating the implications of changing their orientation. Original coding took several weeks in summer 2024 and produced several individual globes. These were consolidated into a single app in September 2025 based on a shared, extensible framework, with celestial and solar rotation added using AI.

HestiaViz Redux

A revamped version of the HestiaVis tool for visualising place references in the Histories of Herodotus. The original version is no longer operational.
